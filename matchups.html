Alphabetical Hwei APC matchup notes

Aphelios

2/10

Aphelios is a difficult champion to play against. Before the lane, ensure that you know what ALL of his guns do. He starts the game with red/green. As with all lanes, we need to give priority and back up. Don’t try to push the lane. He heavily outranges us with green gun. Once we hit level 3, we can start to play the game. Look for long range poke, we don’t really need to save our E against Aphelios, and can look for more aggressive trading patterns. Of course, depending on his support, you may need to save your E/W for more defensive use cases, though Aphelios himself isn’t too worrying. You outrange Aphelios abilities, with the exception of his Green Q and Ultimate with your entire kit. This is a lane that you can win at level 6 once you have some ability power. Aphelios is very squishy, UNLESS he has red guns, which heal him a lot. Consider getting anti-heal after your first item if his support has significant healing. Bad Aphelios players will just get poked out. Maintain distance and you will be just fine in this lane.

Recommended Summoners
Flash + Teleport setup for standard bot-lane Hwei lanes.
Recommended Runes
Comet scaling page: Sorcery primary with Precision secondary.

Ashe

5/10

Ashe wins the lane through her oppressive levels 1-5. She has a really bad midgame and struggles to win fights when she gets hit by spells. That is to say, she is MASSIVELY outdamaged by you after level 5. Ashe’s main threat comes from taking an all-in with lethal-tempo + her passive. Avoid engaging with her AT ALL when at a disadvantage (this includes pre level 6). When we are even or ahead, we can easily fight her and win. Of course, this depends on support pairing, but that is generally the case. Ashe is not a great champion against Hwei, as she struggles to get in range of us even if we are slowed. If Ashe does attempt to run you down, drop EW in her pathing, and use WQ to flee if needed. Generally though, if you don’t make a massive positioning mistake, Ashe won’t be able to get onto you.

If Ashe uses her ultimate, you can use WQ and walk sideways to dodge it easier. Most people don’t use Hwei’s WQ for this, but it does have some decent width which enables you to be very slippery walking side to side.

Recommended Summoners
Flash + Teleport setup for standard bot-lane Hwei lanes.
Recommended Runes

Aurelion Sol

5/10

Aurelion is such a weird champion in the botlane. First, his stacking mechanic makes him extremely overpowered in the late game. We need to avoid letting him place his E (black hole) on the wave and killing it. Try to save your combo to punish him doing this. He is literally forced to stand still when he channels his Q on the wave, giving you the freest EE+QQ/QW combo of your life. The only thing we need to be worried about is him flying at us when we are out of position. His ult is really good at catching people at the worst moment. As such, be cautious when you don’t have your E (which can interrupt him flying) or when you are separated from your support. Typically don’t use EE when he is flying at you, I prefer to use EQ or EW as those are harder to miss against a fast-moving target.

Brand

7/10

Brand is a rare sight in botlane. When playing against Brand, you are in for a fairly tough lane. The primary advantage we have over Brand is the difficulty Brand has hitting his Q. You essentially NEED to dodge it in order to win the lane. Here are some key tips to always follow against brand:

1His E bounces really far off the wave. With Scorch+DFT, it does a lot of damage. Always move away from the wave when he is looking to hit you with this.

2His W outranges you. Respect this range. Once he misses his W, that’s when you can look to play a bit more aggressive

3Brand cannot kill you in one combo, typically even with ultimate. This makes your life kinda easy. Even if he hits a combo, you can always base (even if on a bad timer). This is a lot better than dieing

4Brand’s weakness is that he is immobile and easy to dive. This is a very simple thing to exploit. Just let your team handle it and follow up. I know it feels like you have low agency to impact that matchup, and you do. Outside of Brand messing up and eating too much poke, he kinda wins until your team engages.

5Rylais is broken on Brand and makes his spells super easy to hit. He also applies it for 4 seconds which is disgustingly strong. Don’t try to 1v1 him if on equal items and he has Rylais.

Caitlyn

7/10

Caitlyn is very glass cannon. Super squishy, but does a lot of damage. Most new mage players struggle against Caitlyn as her auto attacks give her significant range. Couple of things to remember here.

1Caitlyn as a champion heavily rewards fundamentals. She is extremely squishy and when off-tempo, her champion kinda sucks. What does that mean?

Well, if you can manage to knock Cailtyn off her game, shoving her under tower, even killing her once, the champion is honestly terrible. But a Caitlyn from ahead is extremely oppressive and can snowball very effectively.

We outrange Cailtyn with our basic spells, but her Q is the longest range spell in this lane. It’s easy to dodge though, so you shouldn’t get hit by it too often. Outside of this, always watch out for Caitlyn traps and play from range. Caitlyn’s weakness is being outranged, so just sit inside of your spell (but not her auto range) to win trades.

The weakness of Corki is his short range and weak scaling (though if he gets a lead, he becomes a monster). Some advice to stay safe against Corki:

TipNever take an all-in fight until you have a significant lead or can 1 shot the Corki

TipKeep your E off-cooldown (don’t trade with it often). We need to save this for when Corki tries to W forward to trade on you.

If you can manage to just QQ the Corki from range, you will succeed. Otherwise, play off of your supports spells, and stay safe. We definitely outscale in this lane, I would recommend playing safe until around level 7-9 when you start to get more AP and points in spells.

4/10

Jhin’s movement speed makes him very strong against mages. It’s not too difficult for him to dodge spells. Always wait until he is an auto attack animation before trying to hit him with spells. Wait until level 5-6 and you will start being more useful than Jhin inside trades. This is a Hwei favored lane and your champion does generally outscale his inside of teamfights. You win the 1v1, you are stronger inside teamfights, just make sure that you don’t miss your spells due to his high movement speed.

Once Jhin is level 6, his wincon is to hit with a couple of auto attacks and spells, back up and press his ultimate to finish you off. So just don’t engage him at this point, only trade from super far away and use your health sparingly. If you are below half HP you are essentially dead. If he does press his ultimate and you’re in lethal range, use your WQ to run around side-to-side and dodge the shots.

As such, maintain that range and always flash away if he flashes forward. Maintain distance, play very calm League of Legends, and you can win this. Note that he is super immobile and you always have the strong wincon of your support/jungler landing CC that you can follow up on.
