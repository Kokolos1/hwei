Mid Game & Teamfighting

Once towers fall around the 14–15 minute mark, the structured laning phase gives way to a fluid, dangerous mid game. Learn how to sideline safely, respect global threats, and position correctly in teamfights.

Sidelaning

Your role once the lane phase ends
The Role Swap

During the mid-game, your bot-lane duo will typically rotate to farm the midwave while you take either the bot or top sidelane. Your playstyle shifts dramatically — you are no longer an oppressive lane bully. You are a long-range poke mage trying to farm safely in a much longer, more exposed lane.

Assassins are online with their first items. The lanes are longer. One mistake means death. Your job is to clear waves efficiently, avoid overextending, and return to your team before any major objective spawns.

Priority: Getting extra gold from farming is great — but dying in the sidelane is always worse. When in doubt, clear and back.

Global Threats

Ultimates that can reach you across the entire map
Check Before You Extend

Before farming deep in the sidelane, always ask: does the enemy have a global ultimate that is currently available? If you don't know their location and their ult is up, assume they are already on the way to you and play accordingly.

Twisted Fate Nocturne Ashe Pantheon Shen Ezreal R

Track their cooldowns on the scoreboard. Most global ultimates have a 120–150 second cooldown. Once you see it used, you have a clean window to extend and farm safely.

How to Play Around Globals
  • 01Keep a ward in the river so you see the jungler rotating — this also catches some global cast animations early.
  • 02Hug closer to your tower whenever global ults are up and you cannot see the enemy.
  • 03Ping your team the instant you see a global ultimate fire — they need the warning too.
  • 04Note the cooldown once used. Farm aggressively in that window, then pull back again before it comes back up.

Laning vs. Toplaners

When their toplaner swaps into your sidelane
Bruisers in the Sidelane

If the enemy toplaner swaps into your sidelane during the mid-game, this becomes significantly more dangerous than facing any midlaner. Most toplaners are melee bruisers who can kill you extremely quickly if you ever enter their melee range. Mel does not have the burst to kill these tanky champions unless she is very far ahead.

The correct play is straightforward but strict: clear waves from maximum range, stay safe under your tower, poke when free, and never play aggressive unless a teammate is present or a kill is guaranteed.

Mid-Game Warding

Vision control as the map opens up
Clearing the Midwave

When you are catching the wave as it crashes mid-lane, place wards in both river entrances. This prevents the enemy jungler from sneaking up behind you while you clear.

River Wards
Standard River Coverage

Ward both the top and bottom river bushes. The jungler can approach from either side — cover both if you have the wards available.

Farming Near Your T2 Tower

If you are catching the wave closer to your own T2, shift wards into your own jungle. This gives you warning if the enemy team rotates to dive you under tower.

Jungle Wards
Defensive Coverage

Wards in your own jungle reveal enemies coordinating a dive — buying you time to back off before they reach tower.

Honest reality: You will never have enough wards to cover every angle. Supplement vision with active minimap tracking. Successful sidelaning is mostly about knowing where the enemy team is.

Mid-Game Teamfighting

Positioning, priority targets, and when to commit
Rule #1 — Should We Even Fight?

Too many games are thrown in objective fights that are clearly unwinnable. Before committing, assess the state of the game. If the enemy has a player with two full items and nobody on your team can kill them in a reasonable window, you will lose that fight unless they make a catastrophic error. Do not be afraid to cede an objective when the math does not work in your favor.

Example: Enemy Ezreal has 2 full items and your team has no realistic way to kill him → concede the dragon and reset. Forcing this fight is one of the fastest ways to throw a winning game.

How to Teamfight as Mel
  • 01Stay in the backline. Let your frontline engage and absorb damage. You are a backline poke mage — do not be the first to enter the fight.
  • 02Target the highest priority in range. Focus a fed carry or their damage dealer — not the tank walking toward you.
  • 03Match your team's focus. If your ADC is hitting the same target, collapse together for a fast kill. Splitting targets extends the fight unnecessarily.
  • 04Watch your flanks. Standing far back means nothing if an assassin like Qiyana comes from behind. Always track where the enemy dive threat is before the fight starts.
  • 05Communicate. Ping fights you cannot win. Tell your team when to retreat. Calm, clear information wins more games than mechanics alone.
Identifying Priority Targets

At any given point in the mid-game, one player on each team typically has a significant gold lead. Stay aware of who this is on both sides. A fed enemy is simultaneously the highest kill priority and the most dangerous person to be near. A useless enemy is the lowest priority — do not waste cooldowns on a 0/5 support when their ADC is fed and chasing your carry.

Fed carries → highest kill priority, highest danger 0/5 enemies → ignore if possible, do not waste spells
