Mid Game & Teamfighting

Lane assignments shift, objectives define the flow, and one good teamfight can swing the entire game. Master every piece of the mid game as Mel.

How Lanes Change at Mid Game

During the mid game, we will be swapping to lane inside the midlane, while our sololaners will occupy the sidelanes. It's important that we keep the waves pushed in midlane and manage resources properly. It's our job to appear at/carry every single fight, so placing ourselves in the midlane makes our job easier as we are now centrally located on the map.

Midlaner vs. Toplaner — Very Different Threats

First, identify who you are laning against. If it's the enemy midlaner, we need to know their kit and how they can trade. Syndra is much scarier (due to her CC) than a Jayce for instance. If it's the enemy toplaner, this becomes much trickier. The majority of toplaners are melee bruisers (Fiora, Renekton, Riven, Ambessa) who can kill us quickly in melee range. Usually though, you will lane against the enemy botlaner which will make getting mid priority relatively easy. Watch the video in the next section for a full rundown on this.

On mid waves, USE CAUTION clear waves, stay under tower, land poke from safety if needed. Midwaves are where most botlaners die wayyy too much. Do NOT step up without confidence that you won't die on the wave.
Mid Game Warding

Generally, we use our blue trinket to either spot enemies walking through a corridor (Ex. the small path to dragon), to spot an enemy camping a bush, or to see if the enemy is on an objective. Blue/white circles in the image to the left are the most common blue ward locations.

You will rarely have enough wards to cover all angles- track the enemy team on the minimap and play accordingly.

Matchup Assessment

How to evaluate your lane opponent in real time during mid game
Reading the Enemy Laner

Even after laning phase ends, you need to evaluate how threatening your current lane opponent is. Have they finished their first item? Are they fed? Are they a champion who spikes at this point in the game? Honing this skill will come with playing more games of Mel and League in general, but it's important to always be thinking of your opponents current strength.

How Aggressively Can You Trade?

If you have been winning lane and know your opponent's kit, you can take confident short trades. Poke, back off, repeat. If you are laning against an unfamiliar opponents, respect their damage completely. For instance, many bruisers will all-in you the moment you overstep even an inch. A good example is Renekton who can flash stun and kill you without any counterplay.

The strength of any mage, including Mel, is their range. Use it to poke and clear waves from a distance. If they close the gap, that's when you E, W, and back away or all-in them.
Useful Questions to Ask Each Wave
  • Is my W up? Can I absorb a trade safely?
  • Do I know where their jungler is?
  • Is the enemy laner ahead or behind in gold?
  • Am I close enough to my tower if they engage?
  • Does killing them here actually help my team?

Assessing Threats

Identifying the most dangerous enemy at any given moment
📸 Threat Assessment Visual
Who Is the Biggest Threat Right Now?

In almost every mid game, one player on each team will have a clear gold lead and be a genuine threat to end the game on their own. Whether it's the enemy toplaner with 10 kills or the enemy ADC with two full items. You must always know who that player is. In tandem with their strength, they will usually have a shutdown. Your goal is to shut this player down, saving spells for them and always being cognisent of their position on the map.

Useless vs. Threatening Enemies

The flip side is equally important: some enemies may be so far behind that they are essentially out of the game. A Kassadin at 2/8 with no items is not a threat. A Zed at 7/1 with full Duskblade is a kill threat on every member of your team.

Knowing who you can ignore is just as valuable as knowing who to focus. It lets you commit your W and your positioning to the fights that actually matter.

✕ Do not tunnel on a low-HP enemy if the fed carry is free to kill your team
✕ Do not burn flash to kill a useless opponent. Your flash is your most valuable tool.
Every fight: Think about the locations of the enemies that matter. If you don't know where their strongest member is (fed Riven, Zed, etc.), then position conservatively and assume they are flanking.

The Golden Rule: Should I Fight?

The most important question you will ask in the mid game

Ask This Before Every Objective Fight

The answer will often be yes, but sometimes the answer will be no. It is too often that games are thrown in a dragon fight that is clearly not winnable. If the enemy Ezreal has a full 2-item powerspike and nobody on your team has the damage to kill him, you will lose the fight unless the Ezreal does something horribly wrong. Do not be afraid to give objectives in conditions like this.

FIGHT ✓ Your team has two items each, the enemy ADC is split farming top, and Dragon is about to spawn. 5v4 with vision- contest it.
FIGHT ✓ The enemy jungler just died bot, Baron spawned 20 seconds ago. Free Baron- take it.
GIVE ✗ Enemy Syndra is 8/1 with Luden's + Shadowflame. Nobody on your team can kill her. Give Dragon- it's not worth 5 deaths.
GIVE ✗ Your have zero items because you died 4 times in lane. Fighting Dragon in a 5v5 is a loss- you are playing 4v5 effectively.
If You Decide to Fight

Our job as a mage is to stand far back, use spells from range, and only target squishy enemies who find themselves overextended. With proper positioning, we stand back and allow our frontline to engage and tank damage for us. Target the highest-priority squishy in range, or the same target your teammates are focusing. Communicate before the fight starts — ping the target, ping your intention. Mage teamfighting requires situational awareness far more than mechanical skill.

05

Mage Positioning & Targeting

Where to stand and who to hit
▶ Teamfight Positioning Video
Stay in the Back

Your frontline is a shield. Let them absorb the engage while you cast from behind. The moment you step in front of your tanks, you become the target.

Watch the Flanks

Standing far back is worthless if Qiyana, Zed, or Kat comes from the side or behind. Before every fight, rotate to face a wall if possible and always know where stealth champions are.

Prioritize Targets

Hit the fed carry or the squishy that is most overextended. Dumping your entire rotation into the enemy tank is one of the most common mistakes mage players make.
