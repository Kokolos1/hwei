Mid Game & Teamfighting

Lane assignments shift, objectives define the flow, and one good teamfight can swing the entire game. Master every piece of the mid game as Mel.

How Lanes Change at Mid Game

During the mid game, your bot lane/support duo will typically be farming the mid waves while you farm either the bot or top sidelane. Your playstyle remains similar regardless of which sidelane you are in, but adjusts based on the specific threats you face.

Midlaner vs. Toplaner — Very Different Threats

First, identify who you are laning against. If it's the enemy midlaner, you already know their kit and how hard they can trade. If it's the enemy toplaner, this becomes much trickier. The majority of toplaners are melee bruisers — Fiora, Renekton, Riven, Ambessa — who can kill us quickly in melee range.

Against tanky bruisers in the sidelane: clear waves, stay under tower, land poke from safety. Mel rarely has the raw damage to duel these champions unless very far ahead.
Mid Game Warding in the Sidelane

If you are clearing waves in the center of the lane, ward the river to spot the enemy jungler approaching from behind. If you are clearing closer to your T2 tower, place wards deeper in your own jungle to catch dives.

You will rarely have enough wards to cover all angles — track the enemy team on the minimap and play accordingly.

02

Global Ultimate Threats

Champions that punish you for overextending in the sidelane
Always Check: Does the Enemy Have a Global?

Before farming any sidelane, ask yourself whether the enemy team has a global ultimate that can reach you while extended. If so, you must never overextend while these abilities are off cooldown. Track them on the minimap and note the cooldown the moment you see them used.

  • Twisted Fate
    Extreme Danger
    Destiny reveals you and teleports him to a nearby location. He will show up with a stun card and you will die. Never farm a sidelane when his ult is up and you have no vision.
  • Nocturne
    Extreme Danger
    Paranoia blinds the entire map and he dives from a massive range. If you are alone in a sidelane without a ward, assume he will come for you. Recall or hug your tower the moment he hits 6.
  • Ashe
    Notable Threat
    Her Enchanted Crystal Arrow travels globally. A well-aimed arrow from across the map while you are farming under tower can stun you long enough for her team to collapse on you.
  • Pantheon
    Notable Threat
    Grand Starfall lands after a travel delay. You will see the indicator — move immediately. Ping his ult to your team every time you see it cast.
  • Shen
    Team Threat
    Stand United does not threaten you directly, but turns a losing fight for your bot lane or jungle into a win. Track when he uses it and exploit the 1v1 window while he is channeling.
03

Matchup Assessment

How to evaluate your lane opponent in real time during mid game
Reading the Enemy Laner

Even after laning phase ends, you need to evaluate how threatening your current lane opponent is. Have they finished their first item? Are they fed? Are they a champion who spikes at this point in the game?

How Aggressively Can You Trade?

If you have been winning lane and know your opponent's kit, you can take confident short trades — poke, back off, repeat. If you are laning against an unfamiliar toplaner, respect their damage completely. Many bruisers will all-in you the moment you overstep by even one step.

Mel's strength in the sidelane is her range. Use it to poke from distances where your opponent simply cannot respond. If they close the gap, that's when you E, W, and back away.
Useful Questions to Ask Each Wave
  • Is my W up? Can I absorb a trade safely?
  • Do I know where their jungler is?
  • Is the enemy laner ahead or behind in gold?
  • Am I close enough to my tower if they engage?
  • Does killing them here actually help my team?

Assessing Threats

Identifying the most dangerous enemy at any given moment

📸
Photo Placeholder

Who Is the Biggest Threat Right Now?

In almost every mid game, one player on each team will have a clear gold lead and be a genuine threat to end the game on their own — whether it's the enemy toplaner with 10 kills or the enemy ADC with two full items. You must always know who that player is.

Useless vs. Threatening Enemies

The flip side is equally important: some enemies may be so far behind that they are essentially out of the game. A Kassadin at 2/8 with no items is not a threat. A Zed at 7/1 with full Duskblade is a kill threat on every member of your team.

Knowing who you can ignore is just as valuable as knowing who to focus. It lets you commit your W and your positioning to the fights that actually matter.

✕ Do not tunnel on a low-HP enemy if the fed carry is free to kill your team
✕ Do not ignore a fed enemy just because they are across the map — they will rotate
Every fight: before casting a spell, locate the fed enemy. If you cannot see them on the minimap, assume they are flanking.
05

The Golden Rule: Should I Fight?

The most important question you will ask in the mid game

Ask This Before Every Objective Fight

The answer will often be yes, but sometimes the answer will be no. It is too often that games are thrown in a dragon fight that is clearly not winnable. If the enemy Ezreal has a full 2-item powerspike and nobody on your team has the damage to kill him, you will lose the fight unless the Ezreal does something horribly wrong. Do not be afraid to give objectives in conditions like this.

FIGHT ✓ Your team has two items each, the enemy ADC is split farming top, and Dragon is about to spawn. 5v4 with vision — contest it.
FIGHT ✓ The enemy jungler just died bot, Baron spawned 20 seconds ago. Free Baron — take it.
GIVE ✗ Enemy Ezreal is 8/1 with Luden's + Shadowflame. Nobody on your team can kill him. Give Dragon — it's not worth 5 deaths.
GIVE ✗ Your ADC has zero items because they died 4 times in lane. Fighting Dragon in a 5v5 is a loss — you are playing 4v5 effectively.
If You Decide to Fight

Our job as a mage is to stand far back, use spells from range, and only target squishy enemies who find themselves overextended. With proper positioning, we stand back and allow our frontline to engage and tank damage for us. Target the highest-priority squishy in range, or the same target your teammates are focusing. Communicate before the fight starts — ping the target, ping your intention. Mage teamfighting requires situational awareness far more than mechanical skill.

Stay in the Back

Your frontline is a shield. Let them absorb the engage while you cast from behind. The moment you step in front of your tanks, you become the target.

Watch the Flanks

Standing far back is worthless if Qiyana, Zed, or Kat comes from the side or behind. Before every fight, rotate to face a wall if possible and always know where stealth champions are.

Prioritize Targets

Hit the fed carry or the squishy that is most overextended. Dumping your entire rotation into the enemy tank is one of the most common mistakes mage players make.

Mage Positioning & Targeting

The full written breakdown of how to play teamfights as Mel
The Backline Principle

Mel's role in every teamfight is the same: she is a long-range damage dealer who should never be in melee range. Use your Q and E from maximum distance. Your passive stacks build up naturally when you are casting on cooldown. Do not panic and walk forward — the stacks will come.

If your frontline is getting destroyed and the enemy is inside your team, do not try to be a hero. Back off, reset the position, and look for a second engagement. Dying never helps your team.

When to Use W in Teamfights

Your W is a reactive tool, not a proactive one. Do not use it at the start of a fight "just in case." Save it for the most dangerous incoming ability — the Malphite ult flying at your face, the Orianna shockwave, the Nautilus hook.

Used at the right moment, your W turns a fight completely. Used too early, you become vulnerable for 35 seconds while every enemy ability is still available to hit you.

Ultimate Timing — Wait for the Glow

Your R executes enemies who have hit Mel's passive threshold. Wait for the golden glow on your HUD and the glowing icon above the enemy's head before casting. Outside of very rare situations where your stacks are about to expire, never cast R early. A wasted ultimate is 100+ seconds of no execute threat.

What to Do When You Get Caught

If an assassin or bruiser gets on top of you, the priority order is: 1) Cast W to block their next ability. 2) Use E to root them or create distance. 3) Flash away if needed. 4) Only auto attack while retreating if it is safe to do so. Do not stand and trade — that is never your win condition.

08

Communication & Flexibility

Pings, callouts, and adapting your plan as the game evolves
Communicate Before the Fight

Ping the target before the fight starts. Ping your intention — are you engaging or retreating? Ping danger if you spot the enemy jungler collapsing. Mage teamfighting requires everyone to be on the same page far more than mechanical individual play.

Be Willing to Adapt

The mid game is the most chaotic phase of the game. Your plan from 10 minutes will not survive contact with a 0/8 Garen who just finished Warmog's. Adapt constantly. If a fight is going badly, ping retreat and regroup. If the enemy is falling apart, push the advantage immediately — do not wait for your "ideal" position.

Ping Her Roams

Every time the enemy midlaner disappears from lane — whether it's Katarina, Talon, or anyone with roam potential — ping immediately and track their movement. Type it in chat if needed. Your teammates will not always look at the minimap. Your warning can save a kill, a tower, or the game.
