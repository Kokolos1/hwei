Late Game

Here's how to close out games as Mel. This phase starts around the 30-minute mark.

Late Game Overview

Late Game Overview

Teamfighting, positioning & priorities at full build
The State of the Game

At this point in the game, most towers have fallen and the game will be over in 1–2 teamfights. Sidelaning is essentially over. Your biggest concern — especially in soloqueue — is that everyone will just be sittinga around midlane and trying to kill you. This happens ALL THE TIME. Do not leave your team alone. And especially do not BE alone. We need to stay with our teamates from this point onward. Only defend or catch sidewaves when absolutely necessary.

You should have 4–5 items at this point. Getting more gold from farming is great, but protecting your team from dying is the highest priority. One mistake can lose the game, so we need to minimize our own while capitalizing on the enemy's. The same rules as midgame apply in this section, except every death can be game ending. As always, I have one main piece of advice. DON'T DIE. Seriously. Just don't die. That is all you need to do. An extra 200 gold is not going to win the game at this point, but not dieing probably will.

Game Length by Elo: Why It Changes Late Game Priorities

The image to the left is a 3-month-old snapshot of game duration by rank. These numbers are basically the exact same today (and I did steal it from a random Reddit post). The important thing to note: games last longer the more you climb.

Why does this matter? Well, ADCs tend to outscale mages, and longer games give them more time to scale. But Evan... doesn't this mean mages are bad in low elo? WRONG.

While it is true that ADCs can outscale mages, mages are a lot safer than ADCs. In a scrappy environment (which lower elo is certainly scrappier), mages have a much easier time surviving. They simply have more tools — and better defensive items — to keep themselves safe.

I just wanted to share this chart with you. It doesn't really change the way you play — I just find it interesting.

Teamfighting — Same Rules, More Extreme

Teamfighting in the late game is the same as mid game, but with bigger stakes. Tanky champions will be nearly unkillable while mages, assassins, and ADCs deal crazy amounts of damage to each other.

Core rule: Stand far back, use spells from range, and only target squishy enemies who overextend. Let your frontline engage and absorb damage. NEVER go in first (including to any bushes, objectives, or the jungle).
Priority Targets & Awareness

Always know who the highest-threat enemy is. Someone with a full build and 10 kills will destroy your team if left unchecked. Focus your damage on the fed carry or whichever squishy target is most in range.

Mage teamfighting seems simple — stand back and poke — but it requires constant situational awareness. Standing far back is useless if the enemy Qiyana flanks from behind and one-shots you. Always know where every enemy is before committing to a fight.

Things to NEVER do in late game
✕Sidelane alone when all 5 enemies are unaccounted for on the minimap
✕Force a fight at Baron/Dragon when you are clearly outscaled or down in resources
✕Waste your W early in a fight — save it for the most dangerous incoming ability
✕Leave your team alone — they will die instantly
✕Use your W and E for damage without confirming you are safe
