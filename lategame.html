Late Game

Most towers have fallen. Champions have 4–6 items. A single teamfight decides the game. Every decision carries maximum consequence — minimize your mistakes and capitalize on the enemy's.

Late Game Overview

The state of the game when towers are gone
What Changes in Late Game

When the game reaches this phase, the structure of the mid-game completely dissolves. Sidelaning is essentially over. Everyone is grouping or should be. Death timers are now so long that losing a single teamfight will likely result in losing the entire game — an inhibitor, a Baron, or the nexus itself.

At this point, you should have 4–5 items. The damage across both teams becomes extreme. Tanky frontliners become nearly unkillable, while mages, assassins, and ADCs are one-shotting each other. One mistake on either side can immediately end the game.

4–5 Items Long Death Timers 1–2 Teamfights = Game Over No More Sidelaning
Your Job in Late Game
  • 01Stay with your team. Do not split off to farm a sidelane unless it is absolutely necessary. The risk of dying alone far outweighs a wave of CS.
  • 02Minimize your own mistakes. In late game, it is more important to not throw than it is to make big plays. Every death is disproportionately punished.
  • 03Capitalize on theirs. One overstep from the enemy carry is your opportunity to collapse and finish the game. Be ready to move the moment you see it.
  • 04Defend before farming. If your team is in danger, protecting them from dying is a higher priority than catching a sidewave.

ADC Management & Soloqueue Reality

The thing that kills more games than bad teamfighting
The ADC Problem

In soloqueue, there is one thing that ends late games more reliably than poor teamfighting: your ADC will randomly walk into midlane and immediately die. This happens constantly at every elo. It is not something you can prevent directly — but you can position yourself to respond to it, and you can be aware enough to ping and discourage it before it happens.

✕Do not leave your team alone to catch a sidewave when the enemy team is grouped and looking for a fight. The wave is not worth the risk.
✕Do not follow your ADC into a stupid position. If they are walking toward danger, ping them back and hold your own ground.
✕Do not engage a fight 4v5 because you want to "make a play." Wait for your full team or for a clear opportunity.

When Catching Waves IS Worth It

There are moments where catching a sidewave in late game is correct — but the bar is high. Only extend alone if:

  • ✓You have full vision of the enemy team's location and they are on the opposite side of the map.
  • ✓No major objective (Baron, Dragon, Elder) is spawning in the next 60–90 seconds.
  • ✓The wave will crash or decay otherwise — losing the CS and the push simultaneously.
When in doubt, group. A missed wave is recoverable. A lost teamfight in late game often is not.

Late-Game Teamfighting

Same principles as mid game — but with far higher stakes
Everything Is More Extreme

Late-game teamfighting follows the same principles as the mid-game — stay in the backline, target priority enemies, communicate — but all of the stakes are multiplied. Tanky champions with 4–5 items can feel completely unkillable. Mages, ADCs, and assassins are one-shotting each other. A single positioning mistake can immediately lose you the game.

This is not the time for flashy plays. Minimize your own mistakes and wait for the enemy to make theirs. You will have more opportunities to capitalize on their errors if you are still alive to see them.

Late-Game Teamfight Checklist
  • 01Full team before engaging. Never start a fight 4v5 in late game. The numbers advantage is worth more here than at any other point.
  • 02Know who can kill you instantly. At 4–6 items, the enemy carry or assassin likely one-shots you. Track their position before the fight starts.
  • 03Save W for the most dangerous spell. In late game, one reflected ultimate can instantly swing the entire fight. Do not waste it on a small poke ability.
  • 04R only when the execute icon glows. Your passive stacks are worth significantly more at 4+ items. Wait for the golden glow on your HUD before pressing R.
  • 05Disengage when it goes wrong. If two teammates die in the first three seconds, the fight is likely lost. Flash out and regroup rather than dying trying to 3v5.

Late-Game Warding

Swapping to Farsight Alteration
The Trinket Swap

At this point in the game, swap your Warding Totem for Farsight Alteration. This blue trinket has a much longer range than a regular ward, allowing you to check dangerous bushes and contested areas from the safety of your backline. It does not last as long as a yellow ward, but its range makes it invaluable for objective setup and ambush detection.

Objective Setup
Before Baron / Dragon Spawns

Keep Farsight off cooldown for each objective that is about to spawn. Use it to check the bushes where the enemy team might be hiding for an ambush. Your jungler can also use your blue ward vision to smite-steal a low-health objective without needing to walk into range first.

Objective Not Spawning
When the Map is Quiet

If no major objective will spawn for more than 2 minutes and Farsight is ready, use it like a yellow ward — place it anywhere that might save a teammate from a gank, or in a position that reveals an overextended enemy for a potential pick.

Key habit: Never waste Farsight on an area you can see with normal vision. Save the charge for blind spots near objectives, enemy jungle entrances, or choke points before a fight.
