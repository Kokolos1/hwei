Late Game

Towers have fallen, death timers are punishing, and one mistake can end it all. Here's how to close out games as Mel.

Late Game Overview

Teamfighting, positioning & priorities at full build
The State of the Game

At this point in the game, most towers have fallen and the game will be over in 1–2 teamfights. Sidelaning is essentially over. Your biggest concern — especially in soloqueue — is that your ADC will randomly run into midlane and immediately get killed. This happens ALL THE TIME. Do not leave your team alone. Only defend or catch sidewaves when absolutely necessary.

You should have 4–5 items at this point. Getting more gold from farming is great, but protecting your team from dying is the highest priority. One mistake can lose the game, so we need to minimize our own while capitalizing on the enemy's.

Teamfighting — Same Rules, More Extreme

Teamfighting in the late game is the same as mid game, but with bigger stakes. Tanky champions will be nearly unkillable while mages, assassins, and ADCs deal crazy amounts of damage to each other.

Core rule: Stand far back, use spells from range, and only target squishy enemies who overextend. Let your frontline engage and absorb damage — do not go in first.
Priority Targets & Awareness

Always know who the highest-threat enemy is. Someone with a full build and 10 kills will destroy your team if left unchecked. Focus your damage on the fed carry or whichever squishy target is most in range.

Mage teamfighting seems simple — stand back and poke — but it requires constant situational awareness. Standing far back is useless if the enemy Qiyana flanks from behind and one-shots you. Always know where every enemy is before committing to a fight.

Do I Even Need to Fight?

The first rule of late game: ask yourself if you should be fighting at all. It is too often that games are thrown in an objective fight that is clearly not winnable. If the enemy Ezreal has a full 2-item powerspike and nobody on your team can kill him, you will lose the fight unless he plays horribly wrong.

Do not be afraid to give objectives when the conditions are clearly unplayable for your team. A free dragon given up is far better than a fight that wipes your entire team and hands them a Baron and the game.

Things to NEVER do in late game
✕Sidelane alone when all 5 enemies are unaccounted for on the minimap
✕Force a fight at Baron/Dragon when you are clearly outscaled or down in resources
✕Waste your W early in a fight — save it for the most dangerous incoming ability
✕Leave your ADC alone in base or rotating — they will die instantly
✕Throw your E into a cluster of enemies without confirming your W is off cooldown

Late Game Warding

Farsight Alteration — vision control that wins games
Swap to Farsight Alteration

By the late game, your Warding Totem should be swapped for the Farsight Alteration (blue trinket). This is your primary vision tool for the rest of the game. Keep it off cooldown heading into every major objective window.

Objective Timing
Farsight Before Every Objective

Always have your blue trinket charged and ready before Baron, Elder Dragon, or Dragon spawns. Use it to clear bushes where enemies might be hiding or to give your jungler vision to smite-steal a low-HP objective.

Between Objectives
Using Farsight When No Objective Is Up

If no major objective spawns for over 2 minutes and your trinket is off cooldown, use it similarly to a yellow ward — place it in any area where the vision might save a teammate from a gank or reveal an overextended enemy you can pick.

Bush Control
Checking Enemy Ambush Points

Before your team walks through any chokepoint or river corridor, farsight the bushes ahead. A single ward can prevent a catastrophic engage that wipes your team and loses the game.

Late Game Vision Philosophy: You do not have enough wards to cover the entire map. Stop trying. Instead, focus your trinket and control ward usage on the specific objective window you are preparing for. Farsight one bush. Yellow ward another. That is enough to make smart decisions — you are not expected to have full map vision.
