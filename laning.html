Early Game

Level-by-level laning plan with text and video for each phase
Level 1

For level 1, play for control rather than damage races. Stay at max auto range, last-hit late so the wave stays centered, and only step forward when the enemy commits to a minion. The goal is to exit the first wave with clean CS and enough health to contest level 2.

  • First WaveUse short forward steps to threaten, then reset back. This keeps pressure without giving a free all-in angle.
  • Trade RuleTake quick one-hit pokes when the opponent is locked into a last-hit, then immediately kite out.
  • Level 2 PrepTrack who reaches level 2 first and back off early if you are late so you do not lose lane on the spike.
Level 1 Video
Levels 2-5

This is your primary setup window. Build pressure through repeatable poke patterns and punish predictable movement around minion last-hits.

  • Trade PatternUse short, controlled trades and disengage after landing a clean spell sequence.
  • CooldownsOnly commit when enemy mobility or key defensive tools are unavailable.
  • Wave StateCrash and reset when you gain lane control; avoid overextending without vision.
Levels 2-5 Video
Level 6-10

Play around ultimate windows, objective timers, and wave tempo. Your focus shifts from pure lane chip to coordinated pressure and conversion.

  • All-InLook for kill setups when key enemy cooldowns are burned or when your support can lock targets.
  • TempoShove safely before river movement so you reach skirmishes first.
  • DisciplineIf no clean angle exists, maintain control through poke and vision instead of forcing.

Warding

Vision control as mid lane
River Bush
Top River Bush (Dragon Side)

Ward the top side river bush at 1:30 before the wave hits. This covers the standard level 2 invade path and gives you early warning on jungle position to plan your level 2 trade.

Pixel Bush
Pixel Bush (Both Sides)

Pixel bushes on both sides of mid river give you full vision of enemy jungle pathing. Drop these when you have priority. Critical for tracking invades and roam timing.

Enemy Jungle
Enemy Blue/Red Side Jungle Entrance

When your team has mid priority and you're shoving the wave, deep ward into the enemy jungle entrance to track camps and predict ganks before they arrive at mid.

Dragon / Baron
Objective Vision Control

At 4:30 start building vision toward Dragon. Ward the dragon pit entrance and the connecting bushes. Having vision here 30 seconds before objective spawn gives your team full control of the fight setup.

Sweeping: Always sweep the mid river and lane bushes before extended trades or all-ins. A hidden enemy ward tips off your opponent and lets them dodge your combo setup.
