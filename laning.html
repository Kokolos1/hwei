Early Game

Level-by-level laning plan with text and video for each phase
Level 1
Opening Wave

Level 1 plan: stay safe and avoid bad fights. ADCs are stronger during this point. Set up your level 3-5 trading by staying high HP.

  • For level 1, we are almost always going to give priority. This means to stand back and let the enemy push the wave into us. Hwei (and mages in general) are really really weak level 1. Especially against ADCs that take lethal tempo.
  • Once we hit level 2-3 and are no longer at a level disadvantage, we can look to take harder trades.
  • At the start of every lane, path IMMEDIATELY to the middle brush and drop a ward (video has more details). This will prevent the opponents from being able to bush cheese us level 1.
  • After dropping the ward, backup and play for poke. Wait for the wave to crash and play safe. The goal is to enter level 2-3 with enough HP/mana that we can contest trades after.
  • Make sure not to give CS and XP for no reason level 1. Sometimes though a champion like Nautilus might be able to zone you off the wave entirely.
Takeaway: reach level 2 with enough health, mana, and experience to contest the lane going forward

Levels 2-5
Stabilize

Levels 2-5 plan: play by matchup, stay stable, and avoid falling behind before your level 6 spike.

  • This part of the lane is going to depend HEAVILY on your lane matchup. Check out the matchup tab for more concrete information.
  • Generally speaking, Hwei is decently strong in this window, but loses all-in/extended trades against ADCs. Some ADCs though, such as Ezreal or Varus, break the rules of the lane and will win during this period. Continue to scale.
  • Key to this stage is ensuring that you can assist your jungler with securing scuttle crab, or pinging your jungler to leave crab if you cannot contest.
  • By far the most important thing during this part of the lane is TO NOT DIE. Whatever you do, do not put yourslef behind.
  • Your time is coming to win the lane, but if you aren't confident and your support isn't landing anything for you to play off, just play for poke. Hwei is not the strongest champion right now, but oh boy does it get better at level 6.
Takeaway: if the wave is bad, your support is late, or your lane is in a bad state, don't fight and ping your jungler to give scuttle. Let your jungler/support die if they take bad fights.

Level 6-10
Take Space

Level 6-10 plan: THIS is the period of the game where you can really abuse your champion to the fullest. Even against a champion such as Draven, Hwei should be winning at this point.

  • Starting around level 6-7, you have everything that you need to succeed. Points in all of your spells, several points in your Q, and a mana item such that you will NEVER be worried about going OOM if you aren't excessively spamming.
  • We need to play from range and know our matchup. Against most champions, we can simply 1v1 them even if we are tanking autos. Check the matchup section of this guide for more information.
  • In low elo this lane is still going to be a 2v2, in which case we want to play off our supports CC and take almost all fights. Be sure that you don't get caught by the enemy support though, as that's really the only way for you to die.
  • In high-elo, both supports will often be roaming, and this is our time to shove waves and poke the enemy under tower, abusing our superior 1v1.
Takeaway: 1v1 matchup begins. You are at your strongest and need to look to abuse your range/power spike against your opponent.

Warding

Vision control

But here are the wards that I do place (whenever I remember). Also I used these images from a Mel game. Sorry Mel haters.

Level 1 Ward

We drop this ward level 1 at the start of every game. This prevents the enemy from bush cheesing us and allows us to poke them easier level 1. Immediately sprint to botlane at game start to drop this ward ASAP.

Boring ass river ward

I'm going to be real, junglers can gank you from a lot of angles that aren't straight through river. BUT combining this with a tri-bush ward CAN keep you safe. This ward is especially effective at using the faelight to get vision on drake/enemy jungler taking the scuttle crab.

Tri-Bush Ward

Now, I've shown these bushes on blue side, but they also work on red-side with the opposite ward. Dropping a ward like this can give you vision of an enemy jungler ganking you from this angle. If you're pushed under tower, you can also drop this on your tri-bush to spot if the enemy jungler is moving to dive you. Combine this with the ward above and you can keep yourself safe.

Stealth Champion Warding

For example, warding the river bushes against Shaco doesn't do anything. He is just going to use his Q over the wall. Instead, we want to ward his jungle camps (usually raptors). The same is true for champions like Evelynn and Twitch.

Hwei Farming Guide

How to clear waves, manage mana efficiently & optimize wave management
Standard Wave Clear Pattern

QE + EE and Autos

Hwei can farm incredibly safe and use EE to zone enemies. Standard clearing pattern is QE + EE. Secures prio in essentially every matchup.

Fastest Clear Pattern

QQ + EE and Autos

Hwei typically doesn't use this combo unless NOBODY is around. This is the fastest way to clear waves, but typically not a feasible pattern.

Wave Clear FAQ

Tips & Tricks for Clearing Waves on Hwei.

  • Always be at a wave when it crashes. Clear wave first, roam, place vision, help jungler, etc. AFTERWARDS
  • QE + EE is standard waveclear pattern. Save EE for zoning since the enemy will be forced to respect it's damage/combo with QE.
  • Keep W and E off-cooldown in many matchups. Especially assassin's. It's not worth using these spells if the enemy can kill you once you use them.
  • When looking to base, shove wave ASAP and don't focus on poking the enemy. If you know breakpoints, you can walk away while minions die to QE.
Assumptions

These numbers are practical breakpoints. I didn't include scenarios which are NEVER going to happen.

The math was rechecked against recent Season 16 values. It assumes the following: Doran's Ring, Lost Chapter, Blackfire Torch, Cosmic Drive, and Shadowflame. It assumes Default Comet with one Adaptive Force shard, Q max first, E max second, and the Primary BFT path. Math does NOT assume you use WE, which adds damage. Breakpoints will also hit slightly sooner if you have dark seal (especially if it has stacks).

Melee minions are the hard part

Caster minions are much easier to kill than melees. TLDR: With Blackfire at level 9, QE + EE will typically clear the melees with WE added (only works if you are accelerated). Blackfire + Cosmic is the first stable two-spell full-wave clear.

Cannon minions

These are legit imortal...

Cannon minions are not killable in one combo. The useful thing is how much HP a full combo does. This tells you if autos or another spell is needed. With Q5/E2 & Blackfire, QE + EE + QQ covers roughly 870 HP. Adding WE raises it to about 905 HP. Blackfire + Cosmic + WE does about 1,170 damage.
