Early Game

Level-by-level laning plan with text and video for each phase
Level 1

Respect early burst and focus on clean spacing. Your main goal is to secure CS while avoiding unnecessary health loss before key cooldowns become available.

  • SpacingPlay just outside enemy opener range and step in only when last-hitting is guaranteed.
  • PatienceDo not force long trades. Prioritize lane control and HP preservation.
  • InfoTrack jungle start and lane support positioning before committing forward.
Level 1 Video
Levels 2-5

This is your primary setup window. Build pressure through repeatable poke patterns and punish predictable movement around minion last-hits.

  • Trade PatternUse short, controlled trades and disengage after landing a clean spell sequence.
  • CooldownsOnly commit when enemy mobility or key defensive tools are unavailable.
  • Wave StateCrash and reset when you gain lane control; avoid overextending without vision.
Levels 2-5 Video
Level 6-10

Play around ultimate windows, objective timers, and wave tempo. Your focus shifts from pure lane chip to coordinated pressure and conversion.

  • All-InLook for kill setups when key enemy cooldowns are burned or when your support can lock targets.
  • TempoShove safely before river movement so you reach skirmishes first.
  • DisciplineIf no clean angle exists, maintain control through poke and vision instead of forcing.

Warding

Vision control as mid lane

Ward Placement Philosophy

Warding can be a very confusing concept for most people, and at the most advanced forms of play, can require a lot of coordination. Let's try to keep it simple. We always place a ward that we think can keep us alive if we see the enemy jungler.

Stealth Champion Warding

For example, warding the river bushes against Shaco doesn't do anything. He is just going to use his Q over the wall. Instead, we want to ward his jungle camps (usually raptors). The same is true for champions like Evelynn and Twitch.

Standard Jungler Warding

Against more typical junglers, we want to ward the river bush that the enemy jungler is expected to come from. If we believe that the enemy is clearing from top to bot (80% of games this is true), then we can place a ward on the bottom river bush at 3:00. Why 3:00? Most junglers will fullclear or take 5 camps before ganking, this typically happens around the 3:00–3:30 mark.

Early Game Gankers

Lastly, if we are against an enemy jungler that loves to level 3 gank (Ex. Rek'Sai, Elise) then we want to place an early ward on the side that the enemy jungler begins clearing on (place a ward in top river bush if they start topside).

Sweeping: Always sweep the mid river and lane bushes before extended trades or all-ins. A hidden enemy ward tips off your opponent and lets them dodge your combo setup.
