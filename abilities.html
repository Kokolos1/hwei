Signature of the Visionary

Damaging an enemy champion primes them. Hitting that champion with another damaging ability completes the signature, creating a delayed area detonation. This is why Hwei wants clean two-spell sequences instead of random single-spell poke.

Q
Subject: Disaster
Damage spellbook
Fast poke and front-loaded damage

Devastating Fire

Use when the target is in normal spell range and you need immediate damage. It is the cleanest lane trade spell after a CC setup.

Long-range snipe

Severing Bolt

Best against isolated or immobilized targets, especially low-health enemies. Do not spam it into healthy moving targets unless you are zoning.

Waveclear and zone control

Molten Fissure

Your bread-and-butter wave tool. It clears lanes, blocks choke points, and forces enemies to choose between damage and bad pathing.

W
Subject: Serenity
Utility spellbook
Movement and rotations

Fleeting Current

Use to move between waves, follow a play, disengage with teammates, or create enough speed to land a follow-up control spell.

Shield zone

Pool of Reflection

Drop this when damage is unavoidable or your team is holding a position. It is stronger when allies can stay inside the pool.

Mana and empowered hits

Stirring Lights

Default lane utility when you are safe. It adds damage to your next hits and restores mana, letting you keep spell tempo.

E
Subject: Torment
Control spellbook
Anti-engage fear

Grim Visage

Your most important defensive spell. Hold it against engage supports, divers, and melee carries until they commit, then fear them and reset spacing.

Trap, vision, root threat

Gaze of the Abyss

Place it in corridors, river entrances, and objective setups. It is less reliable in open lane but excellent when enemies must walk forward.

Pull and area setup

Crushing Maw

Use to clump enemies into QE, interrupt movement, or punish champions standing near their wave. It is also a strong peel tool when EQ is not needed.

Expanding slow and delayed burst

Spiraling Despair

Hwei's ultimate attaches to the first enemy champion hit, expands, repeatedly slows nearby enemies, deals damage over time, then bursts. It is strongest when enemies are already crowd controlled or forced through a choke point.

Core Combos

Seven practical patterns with recorded examples
Lane trade

Fissure Drag-Back

QE+EE

Cast QE first to create the damage line, then use EE to pull the enemy back through it. This is one of Hwei's best lane patterns because it works around minion waves, punishes clumps, and makes the enemy's retreat path hurt.

Defensive punish

Fear into Severing Bolt

EQ+QW

Save EQ for the moment an enemy commits forward, then place QW where the fear sends them. It is a simple two-spell punish that lets you stop engage without walking up, especially when the target is already chunked.

Catch punish

Crushing Maw into Severing Bolt

EE+QW

EE gives QW the setup it needs. Pull the target into a predictable location, then drop Severing Bolt before they regain movement. Use this as a clean catch tool against immobile carries or any target already near execute range.

Fast pick combo

Crushing Maw into Fire and Despair

EE+QQ+R

Use EE to drag the target into a fixed spot, then fire QQ during the pull so the damage lands before they can sidestep. Cast R as the control ends to turn a short catch into a real kill threat.

Execution combo

Maw, Despair, Severing Bolt

EE+R+QW

When EE connects, attach R immediately and hold the cursor where the target will be slowed. QW is the finisher here: place it on the ulted target once they are locked down and missing enough health for the bolt to matter.

Trap execution

Abyss Trap into Despair

EW+R+QW

Place EW where the enemy has to walk: river entrances, lane choke points, objective setups, or fogged paths. Once the root triggers, R is the punish and QW is the long-range finish while they are still trapped and slowed.

Peel turn

Fear, Ult, Kite, Snipe

EQ+R+WQ+QW

This is the anti-dive sequence. Fear the champion committing onto you, attach R while they cannot dodge, then use WQ to break the chase angle. QW comes last as they are slowed, separated, and trying to re-enter range.

Note: These are core damage windows, not scripts you force every fight. Use the sequence when the lane state, enemy movement, or objective setup gives you the window.

Leveling Order

Skill Priority

Ability skill order in every role

First max First max- increasing damage & reducing cooldown
Second max Lower cooldown on our primary safety spell
Last max Utility stays useful with fewer points

Q > E > W is the default order for every role. This breakdown is just due to the number increases seen on Hwei's spell. Hwei Q has the most obvious power increases, with Hwei W receiving very little improvements per skill point. Hwei E falls somewhere in the middle, due to the importance of reducing the cooldown on our crowd control spells.

Typical First Three Levels

Level 2
Mana refund & tradesSkilling W second enables mana refund when out of combat, and the easiest trading pattern by using Q-Q followed by a W-E auto.
Level 3
Final Spell to UnlockWe typically unlock our E as our last option. It's pretty greedy to wait to have our CC spell, so skill this earlier if you need it to stay safe.

Use this order as the default. We can also swap E to be our first spell if needed for safety, or against melee matchups where we can easily trade with E-E. I follow this order in 90% of games.
